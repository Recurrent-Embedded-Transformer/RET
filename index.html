Abstract

Our work addresses the critical challenges of low fidelity and limited generalization in co-speech gesture generation. We propose a novel Recurrent Embedded Transformer (RET) architecture for gesture generation, integrating Dynamic Embedding Regularization (DER) with the Vision Transformer (ViT) framework. The RET establishes a gesture generator capable of jointly modeling spatial-temporal dependencies, significantly improving the naturalness and fidelity of generated gestures. The DER strategy enables the model to exhibit better generalization ability for out-of-domain speech data, i.e., zero-shot generation. To overcome the limitations of conventional autoregressive inference (sequential prediction prone to error accumulation and lacking self-correction), we introduce an Iterative Reconstruction Inference (IRI) strategy. IRI progressively reconstructs masked poses through cyclic refinement, augmented by two key components: (1) classifier-free guidance enhances the model's ability to fit the dataset distribution, improving the quality and coherence of generated gestures without requiring additional supervision, and (2) a temporal smoothing process is applied to optimize the smoothness and naturalness of motion, ensuring smooth transitions between consecutive frames and eliminating abrupt or unnatural movements. Experimental results demonstrate that our proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple metrics, most notably reducing the FGD (Fr√©chet Gesture Distance) from 18.70 to 2.48.

The overall pipeline of our work. Given an audio input, our method aims to produce a high-fidelity gesture. We use the loss function LVQ to optimize the compositional VQ-VAEs, enabling them to learn discrete gesture representation. We carefully employ effective data processing strategies to optimize the gesture generator, enabling it to obtain high-fidelity results. In the Inference phase, Si denotes the i-th speech segment. In this phase, we use IRI and a temporal smoothing process. The Recurrent Transformer model is our gesture generator. Additionally, n denotes the number of iterations, which is a variable value depending on the input. The novel inference strategy further enhances the model's performance in a non-autoregressive way.
